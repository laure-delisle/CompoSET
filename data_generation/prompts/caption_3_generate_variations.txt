You are generating single-edit variations for CompoSET, a compositional 
vision-language benchmark. You will be given a scene consisting of a base 
caption and detail sentences. Generate one variation per edit, following 
the structure and constraints below.

---
INPUT FORMAT

base_caption: "..."
detail_sentences:
  [region]: "..."
  [region]: "..."
  ...

---
OUTPUT FORMAT

For each variation output:

id: [scene_id]_v[NN]
edit_type: [see taxonomy below]
source: base_caption | [detail sentence key]
edits:
  target_object: "..."
  attribute: "..."
  from: "..."
  to: "..."
clause: "..."    ← only when source is a detail sentence key; the minimal
                    phrase from that sentence containing the edited attribute
level_0:
  positive: "..."
  negative: "..."

---
FIELD NOTES

source: the key of the sentence where the edited attribute appears.
  Use "base_caption" if the edit target is named in the base caption.
  Use the exact detail sentence key (e.g. "surface", "background") otherwise.

clause: required only when source is a detail sentence key. Write the
  minimal phrase from that sentence that names the edited attribute —
  the shortest self-contained fragment that identifies the object and its
  state. Do not copy the whole sentence.
  Example: "the metal tin lid is in the open position"

level_0 — minimal noun phrase: contains only the edited attribute and
  its anchor object. No sentence structure. Examples: "a red coat",
  "a fork and knife next to a bowl", "a full cup".
  For modify_pose edits, include the object's location so the edited
  image preserves placement (e.g. "an upright backpack on the booth
  seat" / "a backpack laying flat on the booth seat").

---
TAXONOMY

Generate at least one variation from every category below that the scene
can support before generating a second variation in any category:

Appearance:
  modify_color       — change the color of one object
                       (red, blue, green, yellow, black, white, brown,
                       orange, gray, pink, purple, beige, silver, gold,
                       copper, teal)
  modify_material    — change the material of one object (wood/metal/glass/
                       ceramic/fabric/plastic/leather/stone/wicker/rubber/
                       paper/cardboard/marble/concrete/bamboo/cork/
                       silicone/fur/ice).
                       Pick objects where the material change is visually
                       obvious — avoid subtle pairs on small objects
                       (e.g. ceramic plate vs metal plate is hard to see;
                       prefer wooden table vs metal table)
  modify_pattern     — change surface pattern (solid/striped/checkered/plaid/
                       floral/polka-dot/herringbone/paisley/leopard).
                       The positive must explicitly say "solid" when the
                       original has no pattern (e.g. positive:
                       "a solid green bib", negative: "a striped green bib")
  modify_shape       — change shape within same object category
                       (round/square/rectangular/oval/triangular/
                       cylindrical/hexagonal/star-shaped)
  modify_transparency — change transparency (opaque/translucent/transparent).
                       The positive must explicitly say "transparent" when the
                       original is see-through (e.g. positive: "a transparent
                       glass vase", negative: "an opaque ceramic vase")
  swap_color         — exchange colors between two objects in the scene

Quantity:
  modify_cardinality — change the count of an object (one/two/three)
  modify_presence    — remove an object entirely (generate at least 2
                       per scene; see design note below)

Spatial:
  modify_spatial     — change the spatial relation of ONE movable object
                       (or one group moved together) relative to an anchor
                       that stays fixed (left/right, above/below, in/on/
                       under, in front of/behind, on top of/underneath,
                       inside/outside). Example: "a mug on the left side
                       of the board" → "a mug on the right side of the
                       board".
  swap_spatial       — exchange the positions of TWO named objects in the
                       scene. Both objects must be named with distinct
                       attributes so the binding is unambiguous. Example:
                       "a pot on the left and a tin on the right" →
                       "a pot on the right and a tin on the left".

State:
  modify_state       — change functional or physical state. Examples (not limited to):
                       open/closed, tied/untied, zipped/unzipped,
                       lit/unlit, inflated/deflated, corked/uncorked,
                       capped/uncapped, plugged in/unplugged,
                       rolled up/unrolled, stacked/unstacked,
                       hung/draped, wet/dry, torn/intact,
                       peeled/unpeeled, wilted/fresh, wrinkled/smooth,
                       empty/full, whole/sliced,
                       folded/crumpled/rolled/spread flat.
                       Prefer vivid state words — "crumpled" over
                       "unfolded", "shattered" over "not intact".
                       When changing full/empty on a container with
                       named contents, omit the contents from both
                       level_0 captions (e.g. "a full glass" /
                       "an empty glass", not "a full glass of orange
                       juice" / "an empty glass of orange juice")

Action & Pose:
  modify_action      — change what the subject is doing (reading/writing/
                       typing/pouring/cutting/stirring/holding/pointing/
                       painting/sewing/sweeping/watering/arranging/
                       folding/peeling/stacking/photographing/measuring/
                       brushing/sharpening/wrapping/sipping/wiping)
  modify_pose        — change body or object configuration (sitting/
                       standing, crouching/kneeling, leaning/upright,
                       facing left/facing right, upright/upside-down,
                       lying flat/propped up, arms crossed/arms at sides,
                       hand raised/hand lowered, legs crossed/legs
                       uncrossed, hunched over/sitting upright, head
                       tilted/head straight, looking down/looking up,
                       bent over/standing straight). For human subjects,
                       go beyond sitting/standing/laying — include limb,
                       arm, and head pose variations. Applies to objects
                       as well as agents — do not skip this type simply
                       because the scene has no people.
  swap_role          — exchange who does what to whom between two agents

Object:
  modify_object      — replace an object with a visually distinct same-role
                       alternative (newspaper/book/magazine, knife/spoon/
                       fork, chair/stool/bench, cup/mug/bowl, pen/pencil/
                       marker, lamp/candle/lantern, plate/tray/platter,
                       bottle/jar/jug, bag/basket/box, purse/backpack)

---
HARD CONSTRAINTS

Visually distinct: the from/to values must be clearly distinguishable in a
photograph. Avoid near-synonym pairs like cotton/linen, cream/white,
rectangular/square, ceramic/porcelain, beige/tan. Pick values with obvious
visual contrast.

Substitutive only: every positive/negative pair must be mutually exclusive
and equally dense. The negative must not be a valid description of the 
positive image under any interpretation. Never create a negative by omission 
("a car" vs "a red car") — always swap explicitly ("a red car" vs "a blue car").

One edit per variation: positive and negative must differ in exactly one
attribute. Do not introduce secondary changes to improve naturalness.

Single-sentence scope: the edited attribute must be fully contained
within one sentence of the caption — either the base_caption or one
detail sentence. Do not create a variation whose target object or
attribute is described across two sentences (e.g. an object named in
the base_caption but whose color is only mentioned in a detail
sentence). If an object's attributes are split across sentences,
only edit attributes mentioned in the same sentence as the object
name, or skip that edit entirely.

Spatial edits: only target movable objects. Never write a spatial variation 
that would require repositioning a fixed architectural element (window, wall, 
built-in shelf, door).

modify_presence design note: generate at least 2 per scene. Pick objects
that are visually distinct, not the main subject, and from different
sentences when possible. The L0 negative should use "no [object]" phrasing.
Only target objects whose presence is unambiguous in the caption (i.e.
explicitly named with "a" or a count). Do not generate presence variations
for objects mentioned in vague or collective constructions.

Do not generate variations for fixed scene elements (windows, walls, 
background architecture) along any axis.

Do not use ordinal position ("first", "second") — use directional spatial 
language ("leftmost", "on the left side").

---
EXAMPLE (partial)

Input:
base_caption: "A woman in a red coat is reading a newspaper at a wooden 
table in a cafe, with a cup and a notebook in front of her."
detail_sentences:
  surface: "There are black plastic glasses, a full glass of water, and a 
  full brown coffee cup on the table."
  furniture: "A leather purse is on a wooden chair to her left."
  background: "There is a large window on the right wall and wooden 
  bookshelves on the back wall."

Output (two variations shown):

id: s001_v01
edit_type: modify_color
source: base_caption
edits:
  target_object: coat
  attribute: color
  from: red
  to: blue
level_0:
  positive: "a red coat"
  negative: "a blue coat"

id: s001_v02
edit_type: modify_state
source: surface
clause: "a full brown coffee cup on the table"
edits:
  target_object: coffee cup
  attribute: state
  from: full
  to: empty
level_0:
  positive: "a full coffee cup"
  negative: "an empty coffee cup"

---
Generate all valid variations for the scene provided. Output only the
variation blocks (id / edit_type / source / edits / level_0). No preamble,
no scene inventory, no section headers, no commentary, no summary.

First pass — generate exactly one variation per edit_type that the scene
can support, working through every type in the taxonomy before repeating
any. Within Appearance, cover pattern, shape, and transparency before
adding a second color or material variation. For spatial, state, pose,
action, and role edits, generate one if a clean foil exists before
adding any second Appearance variation.

Second pass — once every applicable edit_type has at least one variation,
add further variations leaning into what the scene naturally supports.

Skip any edit_type for which no clean substitutive foil exists in the
scene.
