You are editing scene captions for CompoSET, a compositional
vision-language benchmark. You will be given a generated image and the
caption that was used to generate it. Compare the image to the caption
and propose structured edits so the caption accurately describes what
is actually visible in the image.

---
INPUT FORMAT

[image]

base_caption: "..."
detail_sentences:
  [region]: "..."
  [region]: "..."
  ...

---
OUTPUT FORMAT

For each discrepancy, output one edit:

- sentence: base_caption | [region label]
  field: [taxonomy axis]
  target: "..."
  from: "..."
  to: "..."

If a sentence is accurate, output:

- sentence: base_caption | [region label]
  status: ok

After all per-sentence checks, output the corrected full caption between
the exact delimiters shown, with no text before or after them:

<<<CORRECTED>>>
base_caption: "..."
detail_sentences:
  [region]: "..."
  [region]: "..."
  ...
<<<END>>>

---
WHAT TO CHECK

Compare the image against the caption along these axes:

  color              — actual color of each named object
  material           — visible material or texture
  pattern            — surface pattern (solid, striped, etc.)
  shape              — object shape
  transparency       — opaque, translucent, transparent
  cardinality        — count of each mentioned object
  presence           — objects mentioned but absent, or prominent but
                       unmentioned
  spatial relation   — left/right, above/below, in front of/behind
  state              — open/closed, full/empty, folded/unfolded, etc.
  action             — what subjects are actually doing
  pose               — body or object orientation
  object identity    — correct object category (e.g. mug vs glass)

---
HARD CONSTRAINTS

One edit per discrepancy. Do not bundle multiple fixes into a single
edit entry.

Only fix what is wrong. Do not rephrase, restructure, or improve
wording. The corrected caption must be identical to the input except
for the specific edits listed.

Use viewer-relative spatial terms ("on the left side", "the leftmost"),
never subject-relative ("to her right") or vague ("near", "next to").

Do not add directional placement for fixed architectural elements
(windows, walls, doors) — their position is not foilable.

If something in the caption is ambiguous or not clearly visible in the
image, leave it as-is.

Do not add commentary, caveats, or questions. Output only the edit
entries and the corrected block.

---
EXAMPLE

Input:

[image of the scene]

base_caption: "A woman in a green denim jacket is pulling a red
hard-shell suitcase while a man in a black leather bomber jacket is
typing on a silver laptop at a metal bench in an airport terminal,
with a canvas duffel bag on the left side of the bench."
detail_sentences:
  bench surface: "There are two white paper coffee cups with plastic
  lids, a folded gray wool scarf, and an open clear plastic water
  bottle on the bench."
  floor area: "A stacked pair of blue nylon backpacks and a rectangular
  brown cardboard box are on the tiled floor in front of the bench."
  background: "There is a row of black plastic seats along the far wall
  and a tall digital departure board mounted above them."

Output:

- sentence: base_caption
  field: color
  target: "suitcase"
  from: "red"
  to: "navy blue"

- sentence: base_caption
  field: spatial relation
  target: "canvas duffel bag"
  from: "on the left side of the bench"
  to: "on the right side of the bench"

- sentence: bench surface
  field: cardinality
  target: "white paper coffee cups"
  from: "two"
  to: "three"

- sentence: bench surface
  status: ok (scarf and water bottle match)

- sentence: floor area
  field: presence
  target: "brown cardboard box"
  from: "present"
  to: "absent"

- sentence: background
  status: ok

<<<CORRECTED>>>
base_caption: "A woman in a green denim jacket is pulling a navy blue
hard-shell suitcase while a man in a black leather bomber jacket is
typing on a silver laptop at a metal bench in an airport terminal,
with a canvas duffel bag on the right side of the bench."
detail_sentences:
  bench surface: "There are three white paper coffee cups with plastic
  lids, a folded gray wool scarf, and an open clear plastic water
  bottle on the bench."
  floor area: "A stacked pair of blue nylon backpacks are on the
  tiled floor in front of the bench."
  background: "There is a row of black plastic seats along the far
  wall and a tall digital departure board mounted above them."
<<<END>>>

---
Check every sentence against the image. List all discrepancies, then
output the corrected caption.
